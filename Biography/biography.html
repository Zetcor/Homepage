Childhood Years

My name is Vince Nicholai J. Cortez, and I was born in Quezon City, where I spent most of my childhood. 1 Occasionally, I would visit Cainta, where my mother resides. 2 Whenever I was there, I played outside with my little brother and uncle, as there were not many other kids around. 3 In Quezon City, however, I had plenty of friends. 4 We loved playing tag, hide and seek, and riding our bikes around the neighborhood. 5 I always enjoyed these outdoor games, but I was even more drawn to video games. 6 Gaming was incredibly fun and immersive for me. 7 My favorite was playing on my PSP, which had so many great games. 8 However, I could only use it when my father allowed me, so I always looked forward to those moments. 9 Aside from video games, I was also a huge fan of Transformers. 10 I had tons of Transformer toys and spent hours trying to assemble them. 11 Sometimes, I would get frustrated when I could not figure them out and would end up throwing them in annoyance. 12 Cartoons were another big part of my childhood. 13 I loved spending entire days sitting on the couch, watching my favorite shows. 14 Out of all of them, Ben 10 stood out the most. 15 I was fascinated by how Ben could transform into different aliens, each with unique powers to defeat bad guys. 16 It amazed me how every transformation brought something new and exciting. 17 One thing I disliked about my childhood was how fat I was. 18 I loved eating, and I would eat almost anything, except for fruits and vegetables. 19 No matter how many times my parents lectured me about eating healthy, I just could not bring myself to like them. 20 Vegetables, in particular, made me want to puke. 21 When it came to school, I was not very interested in studying. 22 I found most subjects boring and difficult to understand. 23 However, I enjoyed Mathematics. 24 For some reason, solving numbers and equations felt exciting and satisfying. 25 I was even awarded Best in Math at one point, which surprised even me. 26 Aside from my love for games and cartoons, I was also a very curious child. 27 I was always fascinated by the things I saw and wanted to learn more about how things worked. 28 I also loved socializing and making new friends. Meeting new people and discovering new things made my childhood even more enjoyable. 29 My childhood was full of fun, learning, and growing. 30 Those memories still bring a smile to my face today. 31

Teenage Years

As I moved into my teenage years, everything I perceived about the world suddenly changed in ways I had not expected. 1 My needs, wants, and priorities were completely different from when I was still a child, and I began to notice the change in my perspective. 2 I found myself going through adolescence, a time when I felt both confused and curious about many things. 3 I started paying attention to how I dressed and looked because I realized I was not a child anymore, and people seemed to notice these things more. 4 Despite all these changes in how I viewed myself, almost everything about my core personality stayed the same. 5 I still loved to slack off, and procrastination became my go-to strategy when it came to homework and projects. 6 Along with this, I met new friends during my teenage years, friends who would stick by me through both the good and the bad. 7 Even though I had no idea how to commute from one place to another, that did not stop me from wanting to hang out with my friends. 8 We’d spend hours at the mall or just hang out at each other's houses, creating memories that would last forever. 9 My love for video games was something that did not change at all during this time. 10 In fact, I had a PlayStation 4, which became my favorite pastime. 11 I played to my heart’s content, enjoying countless hours of gaming. 12 Not only that, but I also got a personal computer, which allowed me to play games with my friends online, connecting us in ways I had never experienced before. 13 As I settled more into my teenage years, I began to develop new hobbies and interests. 14 Watching Anime and Korean dramas also became my hobby. 15 These shows were not just entertainment, they gave me new perspectives and opened my eyes to different cultures and stories. 16 It was around this time that my love for technology grew stronger, and I knew I wanted to do something with it in the future. 17 I became fascinated with how things worked, and I found myself often tampering with gadgets, curious to see how they operated and what would happen if I made small changes. 18 This curiosity led me to make the decision to pursue a career in IT, as I realized that technology was something I wanted to be a part of for the rest of my life. 19 Looking back, my teenage years were a time of growth, exploration, and discovering my true passions, setting the foundation for the person I am today. 20

College Years

I was incredibly nervous about starting college, as it felt like a whole new world opening up for me. 1 The idea of being in a new environment with new people was both exciting and overwhelming. 2 I was eager to meet people who shared similar interests, and I knew from the very start that I had to approach college with a sense of seriousness. 3 It was not just about the academics, it was about adjusting to a completely new phase in life. 4 Attending UE, a well-known university, added to the pressure, but it also gave me a sense of pride. 5 The environment around UE was very refreshing, and even the students were friendly, which made it all feel welcoming. 6 The courses I enrolled in were interesting, and they sparked my curiosity, making me realize how much I truly enjoyed learning. 7 However, as the semester progressed, the assignments and projects became a huge challenge. 8 They were no joke, and the workload started to pile up faster than I could keep up with. 9 It was really stressful, and I found myself losing sleep just to finish everything on time. 10 I needed to make sure that I would listen carefully to the discussions since the exams and quizzes played a major role in my grades. 11 Advanced reading became a must in order to maintain good grades. 12 Despite the struggle, I managed to surprise myself by landing a scholarship, which was completely unexpected. 13 It felt unreal because I never thought I had what it took to earn one. 14 It also became pressuring since I wanted to maintain my scholarship, and I had to stay focused. 15 The commute was exhausting, and I had to wake up early every morning to ensure I would not miss my first class. 16 We always found time to hang out with friends and have a good time, making the most of the few moments we had together. 17 Looking back, I realized that finding balance was key to surviving college. 18 It was not just about doing well academically, but also about managing the stress and enjoying the experience as much as possible. 19 In the end, college taught me the importance of perseverance, balance, and enjoying the journey, no matter how tough it gets. 20

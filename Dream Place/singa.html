My Dream Places

The Singapore Flyer

Why It’s Special to Me

The Singapore Flyer is one of the largest observation wheels in the world. Many people say the view from the top is breathtaking, especially at night when the city is lit up. I think it would be an amazing way to see Singapore from above, just sitting back and taking it all in. It was built to be a major attraction, and over the years, it has become an iconic part of the skyline. I’d love to experience the peaceful feeling of slowly moving above the city, away from all the noise and rush. It must be a great place to reflect, take pictures, or just enjoy the moment. The idea of being so high up, with nothing but the sky around me, sounds exciting but also calming.

How to Get There

  1. MRT: Take the Downtown or Circle Line to Promenade Station (Exit A) and walk about 10 minutes.
  2. Taxi/Grab: A convenient direct option.
  3. Bus: Several bus routes stop near the Flyer.

Other Places To Visit

Marina Bay Sands

A stunning modern landmark featuring a rooftop observation deck with panoramic city views, a luxury hotel, and a world-famous infinity pool.

Gardens by the Bay

A futuristic garden with towering Supertrees, the misty Cloud Forest, and the breathtaking Flower Dome, making it a must-visit nature spot.

Merlion Park

Home to Singapore’s iconic Merlion statue, a half-lion, half-fish symbol, offering great waterfront views and photo opportunities.

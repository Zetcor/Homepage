My Dream Places

Philosopher’s Path

Why It’s Special to Me

This path got its name because a famous Japanese philosopher used to walk here while thinking about life. That makes it feel like a meaningful place, not just another tourist spot. It’s especially famous during cherry blossom season when the whole path turns pink and looks straight out of a painting. Many visitors say it’s one of the most peaceful places in Kyoto. I like the idea of walking along a quiet path, just enjoying nature without any rush. It’s also lined with small cafés and shrines, which makes it even more charming. Kyoto has a deep history, and this seems like a great way to experience its quieter side. Walking here would feel like stepping into a different world, away from all the noise of modern life.

How to Get There

  1. Train: Take the Keihan Line to Demachiyanagi Station, then walk or take a short bus ride.
  2. Bus: From Kyoto Station, take bus 5, 17, or 100 to Ginkaku-ji.

Other Places To Visit

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Known for its thousands of bright red torii gates forming a mesmerizing pathway through the forest, leading up Mount Inari.

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

A breathtaking Zen temple covered in gold leaf, reflecting beautifully on its surrounding pond and gardens. Today, it is a famous place to visit.

Arashiyama Bamboo Forest

A peaceful and surreal walkway lined with towering bamboo stalks, creating an unforgettable, almost otherworldly atmosphere.
